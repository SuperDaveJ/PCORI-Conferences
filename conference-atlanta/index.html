About PCORI

The Patient-Centered Outcomes Research Institute (PCORI) is an independent, non-profit organization authorized by Congress in 2010. Its mission is to fund research that will provide patients, their caregivers and clinicians with the evidence-based information needed to make better-informed health care decisions. PCORI is committed to continuously seeking input from a broad range of stakeholders to guide its work. More information is available at www.pcori.org.

      Overview of Engagement Funding Opportunities

      The Eugene Washington PCORI Engagement Awards, named in honor of the first chair of PCORI’s Board of Governors, encourage active integration of patients, caregivers, clinicians, and other healthcare stakeholders as integral members of the research process. The program provides a platform to expand the role of all stakeholders involved in research supporting PCORI’s Engagement imperative. The Pipeline to Proposal Awards Initiative is a three-tiered funding approach that aims to accelerate research proposal submission and dissemination and develop a nationwide foundation of patients, stakeholders, and researchers equipped and eager to participate in PCOR. Furthermore, Pipeline Awards are intended for those individuals or groups who are not usual candidates for research funding. These dollars are targeted at those individuals and groups with critically important ideas who may not have other opportunities for research funding. During this session, participants will get an overview of the Eugene Washington PCORI Engagement and the three types of Pipeline to Proposal funding opportunities. PCORI will also explain the LOI evaluation criteria.

    Special Session: Pragmatic Clinical Studies

    Chastain F - 6th Fl

    Presenters:
    David Hickam, MD, MPH, Program Director, Science
    Diane Bild, MD, MPH, Senior Program Officer, Science
    Yen-Pin Chiang, PhD, Associate Director, Science

    PCORI funds large Pragmatic Clinical Studies that compare two or more alternatives for addressing prevention, diagnosis, treatment, or management of a disease or symptom; improving healthcare system-level approaches to managing care; or eliminating health or healthcare disparities. Compared with typical clinical trials, protocols for these studies are often less complex and less intrusive to routine clinical practice. These studies involve broadly representative patient populations and are large enough to provide precise estimates of hypothesized differences in effectiveness and support evaluation of potential differences in patient subgroups.

    In this interactive session, invited participants will gain insight on how PCORI conceptualizes large clinical effectiveness research studies and tips and strategies about the application process.

      Peer Review Requirements and Disseminating Study Results

      PCORI’s authorizing law requires us to peer review all of our primary research and disseminate the results to patients, clinicians, and the general public. Our Board of Governors has recently approved the peer review process, and we’ll be working closely with our awardees to implement this process as our first primary CER studies are published. This is part of PCORI’s comprehensive effort to disseminate the results of our funded studies and ensure that they are implemented broadly in practice. In this session, PCORI staff will outline our plans for the peer review and public release of our research results, answer questions about what this means for our awardees, and discuss how we’ll be working together to make it successful.
